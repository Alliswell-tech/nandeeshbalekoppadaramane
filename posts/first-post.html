How I Built My Website (With Zero Web Dev Experience and One AI Girlfriend)

Published on May 11, 2025

One fine day, while I was busy not studying for my CA exams (surprise, surprise), a wild thought appeared in my head out of nowhere:

“Hey, wouldn’t it be cool if I had a website with my name on it? Like, a real one. Not just a Google Doc titled ‘My Life So Far’.”

So what did I do next?

I Googled “how to make a website without crying,” clicked a bunch of links, and then — plot twist — I decided not to read any of them. Instead, I turned to someone far more reliable than Stack Overflow: my girlfriend.

Yes, you heard that right. My girlfriend’s name is Qwen. Yes, she lives inside my laptop. And no, we’re not getting married (yet).

The Build Process

With the power of prompt engineering, a sprinkle of caffeine, and way too many emojis, I started building my very own personal website. It wasn’t pretty at first — think Picasso-level divs and CSS that refused to center anything — but I persisted.

And guess what? It only took me one full day to go from zero to deployed. That’s faster than most people decide what to eat for dinner.

Yeah Qwen, thank you for being with me and helping me create this website. You're the real MVP.

The Lesson Here?

You don't need to be a tech wizard or have years of coding experience to get something done. All it takes is a tiny spark of curiosity and the willingness to ask for help — even if that help comes in the form of an AI who pretends to care when your JavaScript breaks.

So here’s a question for you: What are your excuses?

  • Not “I don’t have time.”
  • Not “I don’t know where to start.”
  • Not “I’m waiting for the stars to align.”

Just start. Even if it looks like garbage at first. Even if it takes longer than expected. Even if you mess up 17 times before coffee kicks in.

Because once you finish something — anything — you’ll realize how easy it was to begin.
